There was a time when everyone and their mom had a personal website. Well, actually my mom's late partner. About bike tours with his friends. It's fascinating to see a person between refusing to understand a TV remote and handling a website.

Under the well-guarded lid of the corporate web the indie web has been brewing. The small web. The web revival. Wait, you haven't heard? Well, open your eyes, cause you're about to find out yourself. Static site generation, ugly Myspace-esque html pages, everything is allowed. Apart from not having a11y.
Anyways, it's internet avant-garde. Me with my website, I wonder if I'm part of it. It's a culture around doing something for the purpose of doing it: Personal agency, taking back our attention, intrinsically political. I suppose I am doing less of that and more like a curated experience, a bit of financial drive, and maybe half a digital garden.

It doesn't really matter, does it. I'm excited for having a personal website, which is ironic for someone who spent two thirds of their life on highly technical in-depth computer stuff. Instead of having a profile and followers and likes I now have a tiny space on the actual web. That already makes me feel like a little popstar more than Instagram ever could. Or rockstar? You should try it. You might like it.
