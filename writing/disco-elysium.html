Disco Elysium

Jun 24, 2026 · 6/7 · Excellent · #games/reviews

Disco Elysium. The game that took me longer than any other to finish. 5 years.
What now, a lesson on persistence?

Do you know that feeling, often when reading, that you can’t just keep at it despite being super interesting? The game is a visual novel, detective story about a murder. I’m not the best reader. There’s allegedly a million lines of text in the game, most of which you’ll never see. Or hear. The voice acting is otherworldly. The writing is impeccable, but ironically the book it’s based on was never well received.

Why? It’s grim, hopeless, and without humour. A lesson on humour? While it’s notoriously hard to pull off detective games, (with movies you can at least try to make the viewer and protagonists solve the case simultaneously), it’s been pulled off.

But the studio froze out their original leadership, ZA/UM imploded after having made a masterpiece on failed ideologies and broken institutions.
A lesson on…?

Would I restart the game I would never know how to proceed. Cause it has a way of failing you forward. You progress, even through failed checks, follow another line; The game pushes you forward and you have no idea how it scrambled together the dialogue, RPG systems and non-linearity.
You don’t even have to be correct. In a detective game nonetheless!
A lesson on game-design and mechanics?

If I ended the game 60% in it genuinely wouldn’t have mattered. The whole experience is there from the start. While so much art relies on the plot, it’s just a vehicle here, yet still at the core.
A lesson on the journey mattering more than the goal?

So what’s the lesson, really? Multiple lessons? I don’t think it’s made for lessons. It’s made to he experienced, in whatever way.
